Canh Chua

Description

Canh chua, or sour soup, is a traditional Vietnamese dish that embodies the essence of Southern Vietnamese cuisine. This vibrant soup is a delightful balance of sweet, sour, and savory flavors, typically made with fish, pineapple, and a variety of vegetables. The use of tamarind gives it a distinctive tang, while fresh herbs like cilantro and basil add aromatic freshness. It is often enjoyed with steamed rice and is a staple in many Vietnamese households, especially during the hot summer months. The combination of ingredients not only provides a refreshing taste but also showcases the rich biodiversity of Vietnam's tropical produce.

Ingredients

  • Fish (catfish or tilapia)
  • Pineapple
  • Tomatoes
  • Okra
  • Bean sprouts
  • Tamarind paste
  • Fresh herbs (cilantro, basil)
  • Fish sauce
  • Chili peppers

Instructions

  1. Prepare the fish by cleaning and cutting it into pieces.
  2. In a pot, bring water to a boil and add tamarind paste to create the sour base.
  3. Add the fish pieces and cook until they are tender.
  4. Incorporate the pineapple, tomatoes, okra, and bean sprouts, cooking until the vegetables are just tender.
  5. Season with fish sauce and adjust the sourness with more tamarind if needed.
  6. Garnish with fresh herbs and chili peppers before serving.

*Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy the rich flavors of this traditional Vietnamese dish.
